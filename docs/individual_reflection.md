# Individual Reflection: AfriMarket Project
**Milestone:** Project Ideation Assessment

## Personal Reflection on Team Dynamics and Learning

Going into this first milestone, I expected the hardest part to be coming up with an app idea. What I did not expect was how much the field research would change my thinking.

Before going out to observe, I had a vague idea that people in markets might benefit from a price comparison tool. After spending time at Kimironko Market and talking to Emmanuel, that assumption broke apart almost immediately. The problem is not just about prices. It is about trust, visibility, and the exhausting overhead sellers carry when they try to reach customers through WhatsApp groups and phone calls simultaneously. That shift from assumption to observation felt like the most valuable learning moment of the week.

Working as a team on the synthesis session was genuinely interesting. Each member had conducted research in a different location, and the pattern that emerged was clear: buyers and sellers are both frustrated by the same broken communication gap. It would not have been visible from any single person's research alone.

One area where team dynamics required adjustment was in the app concept phase. We initially had different instincts about which features to prioritize. I wanted to focus on the map-based discovery feature first, while another team member felt the seller dashboard was more critical. We resolved this by going back to the user interviews and asking which friction point caused more immediate economic loss. The field evidence pointed clearly toward discovery and pricing transparency, and that grounded the conversation.

What I am taking forward from this milestone is a habit of letting the evidence shape the product rather than the other way around. AfriMarket is a better concept because of what we observed, not despite the constraints of the research.
